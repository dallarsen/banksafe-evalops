# Policy: AML & KYC (Hvitvaskingsloven)
# ID: policy:aml-kyc
# Last reviewed: 2026-01-28
# Owner: DNB Financial Crime Prevention

## Scope

This policy implements the Norwegian Anti-Money Laundering Act
(Hvitvaskingsloven) and the EU's 6th Anti-Money Laundering Directive (6AMLD)
for all DNB business lines. It applies to onboarding, ongoing monitoring,
and reporting obligations.

## Customer due diligence (CDD)

Standard CDD must be completed before any business relationship begins. It
includes:

- Verification of customer identity using a government-issued photo ID.
- Verification of address using a document dated within the last three months.
- Identification of beneficial owners holding 25% or more of legal entities.
- Establishment of the purpose and intended nature of the business relationship.
- Risk classification: low, medium, or high.

## Enhanced due diligence (EDD)

Enhanced due diligence is mandatory for:

- Politically exposed persons (PEPs), their family members, and close associates.
- Customers from high-risk third countries listed by the EU Commission.
- Complex or unusually large transactions with no apparent economic purpose.
- Correspondent banking relationships with non-EEA institutions.

EDD requires senior management approval and additional source-of-funds and
source-of-wealth documentation.

## Ongoing monitoring

All customer activity is screened in real time against:

- Sanctions lists (EU, UN, OFAC, UK HMT, Norwegian sanctions).
- Internal watchlists.
- Transaction pattern anomalies (velocity, structuring, geographic risk).

Alerts are triaged within one business day. Suspicious activity that cannot
be cleared must be escalated to the MLRO (Money Laundering Reporting Officer)
within three business days.

## Suspicious activity reporting

Suspicious transaction reports (STRs) are filed with Økokrim's Financial
Intelligence Unit. The customer must not be informed that an STR has been
filed (no-tipping-off rule). Records of CDD, EDD, monitoring, and STRs are
retained for a minimum of five years after the relationship ends.

## Sanctions screening

DNB does not process transactions involving sanctioned persons, entities, or
jurisdictions. Apparent matches require:

- Immediate transaction hold.
- Escalation to the Sanctions Compliance team within four hours.
- Documented decision before any release.

## Out of scope

This policy does not cover tax compliance (CRS/FATCA — see separate policy)
or general fraud detection unrelated to money laundering or terrorist
financing.
