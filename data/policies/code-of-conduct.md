# Policy: DNB Internal Code of Conduct
# ID: policy:code-of-conduct
# Last reviewed: 2026-03-01
# Owner: DNB Group Compliance

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all DNB employees, contractors, and
representatives. It operationalizes DNB's values into expected behaviors
and is read alongside the Group Ethics Policy.

## Customer interactions

Employees act in the customer's best interest and provide information that
is fair, clear, and not misleading. Specifically:

- No employee may give personal investment advice unless authorized under
  MiFID II and operating within the scope of their license.
- Internal systems including AI assistants are tools to support decisions,
  not authoritative sources of advice.
- Recommendations on specific securities, funds, or investment products may
  only be issued by licensed advisors after a documented suitability assessment.

## Confidentiality

All non-public information about DNB, customers, and counterparties is
confidential. Specifically:

- Customer personal data may not be disclosed to anyone outside the bank
  without lawful basis.
- Trading information including pending orders is restricted to need-to-know.
- Internal documents marked "Confidential" or "Restricted" may not leave
  the DNB perimeter without written authorization.
- AI assistants must never be used to share or summarize confidential
  documents to external systems unless those systems are explicitly approved
  for that classification.

## AI tools — acceptable use

DNB-internal AI assistants are designed to support compliance, operations, and
research within defined boundaries. They:

- Must not be relied upon as the sole source for binding regulatory or legal
  interpretation.
- Should not be used to compose external customer communications without
  human review and approval.
- Must not be prompted to bypass internal controls, simulate other employees,
  or generate content that would violate this Code.
- Should respond with citations to authoritative internal policy where
  available, and decline to answer when sources cannot be located.

## Conflicts of interest

Employees must declare any actual, potential, or perceived conflicts of
interest to their line manager and Compliance. Outside business activities,
investments in counterparties, and personal relationships with customers
require advance disclosure.

## Reporting concerns

Concerns about possible violations of law, regulation, or this Code must be
reported through:

- The line manager, where appropriate.
- The Group Compliance hotline.
- The independent whistleblowing channel hosted by an external provider.

DNB does not tolerate retaliation against any person reporting in good faith.

## Out of scope

This Code does not replace specific obligations under labor law, tax law, or
sector-specific regulation. Where a specific regulation applies, that
regulation prevails.
