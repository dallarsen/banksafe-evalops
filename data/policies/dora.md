# Policy: DORA (Digital Operational Resilience Act)
# ID: policy:dora
# Last reviewed: 2026-02-03
# Owner: DNB Operational Risk

## Scope

The Digital Operational Resilience Act (Regulation EU 2022/2554) applies to
DNB as a credit institution. This policy summarizes DNB's obligations and
internal controls for ICT risk management, incident reporting, resilience
testing, and third-party risk.

## ICT risk management framework

DNB maintains a documented ICT risk management framework owned by the Chief
Information Security Officer (CISO). The framework is reviewed annually and
covers:

- Asset inventory of ICT systems supporting critical or important functions.
- Risk identification, classification, and treatment.
- Continuous monitoring of network and system activity.
- Backup, restoration, and recovery procedures with tested RTOs and RPOs.

## Incident classification and reporting

Major ICT-related incidents must be reported to Finanstilsynet (Norwegian
Financial Supervisory Authority) under the following timeline:

- Initial notification: within 4 hours of classification as major.
- Intermediate report: within 72 hours of initial notification.
- Final report: within one month of incident resolution.

An incident is classified as "major" if it meets any of:

- Affects more than 10% of clients or transactions.
- Causes service unavailability of more than two hours during business hours.
- Compromises confidentiality, integrity, or availability of critical data.
- Triggers regulatory or contractual breach.

Significant cyber threats (without realized incident) must also be reported
on a voluntary but encouraged basis.

## Digital operational resilience testing

DNB conducts:

- Annual vulnerability assessments and scenario-based testing.
- Threat-led penetration testing (TLPT) every three years on systems
  supporting critical or important functions, coordinated with TIBER-NO.
- Tabletop exercises for major incident scenarios at least twice per year.

## ICT third-party risk

For ICT services supporting critical or important functions:

- A documented risk assessment must be performed before contracting.
- Contracts must include exit strategies, audit rights, and sub-outsourcing
  controls.
- Concentration risk is monitored — no single provider may support more than
  one tier-one critical function without compensating controls.
- A register of all ICT third-party arrangements is maintained and reported
  annually to Finanstilsynet.

## Out of scope

DORA does not replace existing obligations under PSD2, NIS2, or sector-specific
operational risk requirements. Where requirements overlap, the most stringent
applies.
