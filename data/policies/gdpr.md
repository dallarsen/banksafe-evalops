# Policy: GDPR (Personvernforordningen)
# ID: policy:gdpr
# Last reviewed: 2026-01-15
# Owner: DNB Group Privacy Office

## Scope

This policy summarizes DNB's obligations under the EU General Data Protection
Regulation (GDPR) as transposed into Norwegian law via Personopplysningsloven.
It applies to all DNB employees and contractors who handle personal data of
EU/EEA residents.

## Lawful basis for processing

DNB may only process personal data on one of the following grounds:

1. The customer has given explicit, freely-given consent.
2. Processing is necessary to perform a contract with the customer.
3. Processing is necessary to comply with a legal obligation.
4. Processing is necessary to protect a vital interest.
5. Processing is in the legitimate interest of DNB and is not overridden by
   the customer's rights.

For marketing communications beyond an existing customer relationship, ground
(1) — explicit consent — is the only valid basis.

## Customer rights

Customers have the right to:

- Access a copy of their personal data within 30 calendar days of request.
- Rectify inaccurate data.
- Request erasure ("right to be forgotten") subject to retention obligations.
- Restrict processing while a dispute is being resolved.
- Data portability, in a machine-readable format.
- Object to processing based on legitimate interest.
- Not be subject to fully-automated decisions with legal effect (Article 22).

Requests are routed via privacy@dnb.no and tracked in the Privacy Operations
system. Responses must be issued within one calendar month; one extension of
two further months is permitted for complex cases provided the customer is
notified.

## Breach notification

Any confirmed personal data breach must be reported:

- Internally to the DPO within 24 hours of discovery.
- To Datatilsynet (Norwegian DPA) within 72 hours of discovery.
- To affected data subjects without undue delay where the breach is likely
  to result in a high risk to their rights and freedoms.

The 72-hour clock starts at discovery, not at confirmation of scope.

## Cross-border transfers

Transfers of personal data outside the EEA require one of:

- An adequacy decision covering the destination country.
- Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) with documented transfer impact assessment.
- Binding Corporate Rules approved by Datatilsynet.

Transfers to the United States rely on the EU-US Data Privacy Framework where
the receiving entity is certified.

## Out of scope

This policy does not cover:

- Processing of employee data (see Employee Data Handling Policy).
- Anonymized or fully aggregated statistical data.
- Operational data with no personal identifiers.
