# Policy: MiFID II Investment Services
# ID: policy:mifid-ii
# Last reviewed: 2026-02-10
# Owner: DNB Markets Compliance

## Scope

This policy covers DNB's obligations under the EU Markets in Financial
Instruments Directive II (MiFID II), as implemented in Norwegian law via
Verdipapirhandelloven. It applies to all DNB activities involving investment
services, advice, and the manufacture or distribution of financial instruments.

## Client classification

All clients are classified as one of:

- **Retail client** — highest level of protection.
- **Professional client** — meets the criteria of size, expertise, or activity.
- **Eligible counterparty** — typically other regulated financial institutions.

Reclassification requires written request and assessment. Retail clients may
opt up only if they meet two of three criteria: ten significant transactions
per quarter on average over the prior year, a portfolio exceeding EUR 500,000,
or one year of professional experience in financial services.

## Suitability and appropriateness

For investment advice and discretionary portfolio management, DNB must perform
a **suitability assessment** covering the client's:

- Knowledge and experience.
- Financial situation including ability to bear losses.
- Investment objectives including risk tolerance and time horizon.

For execution-only services in complex instruments, an **appropriateness test**
applies — DNB warns the client if the product is not appropriate to their
knowledge and experience.

Pure execution of non-complex instruments at client initiative may proceed
without an appropriateness test, with appropriate disclaimers.

## Product governance

For each financial instrument manufactured or distributed by DNB:

- A target market is defined (positive and negative).
- A distribution strategy is documented.
- Product performance is reviewed at least annually.

Products may not be distributed outside the defined target market without a
documented justification.

## Inducements

Inducements (third-party payments) are only permitted if they:

- Enhance the quality of the service to the client.
- Do not impair DNB's duty to act in the client's best interest.
- Are clearly disclosed to the client.

Independent advice and discretionary portfolio management may not retain
inducements — they must be passed through to the client in full.

## Best execution

DNB takes all sufficient steps to obtain the best possible result for clients,
considering price, costs, speed, likelihood of execution, settlement, size,
and any other relevant consideration. The execution policy is reviewed
annually and on any material change.

## Transaction reporting

Reportable transactions are submitted to Finanstilsynet by T+1 in the format
prescribed by ESMA. Late or inaccurate reports trigger root cause analysis
and remediation.

## Out of scope

This policy does not address insurance-based investment products (see IDD
policy) or pure deposit products outside the scope of MiFID II.
